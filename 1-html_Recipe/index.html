Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 medium brown or white onion, peeled and diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 medium green or red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 cups ripe diced tomatoes, or 2 cans (14 oz. each) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp chili powder (mild)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper (or more to taste-- spicy!)
  • Pinch of sugar (optional, to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 5-6 eggs
  • 1/2 tbsp fresh chopped parsley (optional, for garnish)

Mansaf

Description

Mansaf is a traditional Arab dish made of lamb cooked in a sauce of fermented dried yogurt and served with rice or bulgur

Directions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook until soft, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the lamb and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the bulgur and cook until the bulgur is tender, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the rice and cook until the rice is tender, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the yogurt and cook until the yogurt is tender, about 5 minutes.
  6. Add the salt, pepper, cumin, paprika, cayenne, sugar, and cook until the sauce is thick and the lamb is cooked, about 5 minutes.
  7. Add the tomato paste and chili powder and cook until the sauce is thick, about 5 minutes.
  8. Serve the sauce over the lamb and rice.
